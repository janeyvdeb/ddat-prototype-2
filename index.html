Digital, Data and Technology Profession (DDaT) Capability Framework prototype 2

This Framework describes job roles in the Digital, Data and Technology profession in the Civil Service.

Published 25 July 2019
From:
Government Digital Service

This Framework describes job roles in the Digital, Data and Technology Profession in the Civil Service and provides details of the skills you need.

Roles and job families

A range of jobs and specialisms fall within this Framework and sit alongside related roles in job families (eg the role of data engineer is part of the Data job family).

Please check the following pages for more information on different roles, the levels within roles (eg information on the role of Junior user researcher ranging to Head of user research is included in the User researcher page) and the skills you need to do them.

Success Profiles Framework

The Civil Service uses the Success Profiles Framework to assess whether you have the experiences, abilities, strengths, behaviours and technical / professional skills required for different roles. It goes beyond this Framework in that it tells you about the additional skills and experience you are required both to have and to demonstrate through the recruitment process.

If you are applying for a job in the Civil Service, check the advertised job description, which will tell you exactly what information you need to evidence in order to progress.
